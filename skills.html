Tips and Drills for Skills

Of course, the hardest part of volleyball is getting good at it. Here are some tips from my experience and the best drills for the following skills:
  • Hitting
    1. Make sure you are reaching as high as you can; don't let your arm drop to your shoulder
    2. Keep your hand hard and firm; don't let your fingers soften the contanct.
    3. In a 3-step approach, the first step should be immediate, while the other two are fast and right before you jump.
    4. The best drill for hitting is repetitive setting and hitting from your position.
  • Setting
    1. Get yourself straight under the bag quickly; the ball should be at your forehead.
    2. Get power from your legs, don't just rely on your arms.
    3. Use a soft, diamond-shaped hand position.
  • Defense and Serve Recieve
    1. In defense and SR, the ball is coming at you with so much power, you do not need to provide extra power in your contact. Just use your platform to rebound the ball.
    2. Don't run with your platform. Get to where you need to be and form it at the last second.
    3. Make sure you are balanced before contacting, or your platform won't be steady.
    4. Angle your platform exactly where you want the ball to go, and hold it strong.
  • Serving
    1. Again, make sure you are contacting at your highest reach; don't let your arm drop.
    2. Hold your hand firm and hard to get a good float serve.
    3. Contact the ball on its very core to give it the most power and float.
